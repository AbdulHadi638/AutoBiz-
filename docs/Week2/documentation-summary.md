# Week 2 – Summary

## What I Did This Week

This week was about understanding how a business works before trying to automate anything.

I chose a football turf rental business as my example. I studied how bookings happen, how cancellations are handled, how payments are managed, and how daily operations run.

Instead of thinking about automation tools, I focused on understanding the real steps that happen every day.

---

## What I Learned

I learned that a business process is simply a series of repeatable steps that turn a request into a result.

For example:
- A customer asks for a slot.
- The business checks availability.
- Payment is collected.
- The booking is confirmed.

If these steps are not clearly written down, automation will fail.

---

## What I Created

- A clear business description
- A list of repetitive tasks
- Two detailed workflows (Booking and Cancellation)
- Flow diagrams to visually show the process

---

## Main Takeaway

Automation should only be done after understanding the process clearly.

If the process is messy, automation will also be messy.

This week helped me think like a business owner before thinking like an automation builder.
